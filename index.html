CHAPTER ONE

CALVIN'S JOURNEY

Listen to how Calvin and Angel met.

Calvin Kirby, a 34 year old man from Gallipolis, Ohio, was raised in an abusive household. To tell the story of Sobriety Point, one must understand why its creator, Calvin, needed it in the first place.

Growing up, Calvin experienced a series of traumas that he says contributed to his drug addiction. His mother, who was separated from his father, was addicted to drugs and moved around frequently. Along with living in an unstable household, This left him feeling isolated and in need of help. The only way he knew how to relieve his pain was from using.

What led us to being homeless at first? Drug activity and drug addiction.”


-Calvin Kirby

Listen to Calvin speak about bringing Angel to his level.

(00:29)

When the couple first met Angel was in recovery, but soon after she relapsed. The couple moved to Parkersburg in 2014 where they bounced from friends couches to temporary lodgings and back again. Calvin says he had a temper which made him unbearable for others to be around him, which ultimately led him and Angel to homelessness. Following two drug related arrests, Calvin was offered the option to enter West Virginia drug court rather than serve jail time and the need for stable and sober living situation became an immense and immediate need.

Calvin needed to be in nature, away from the stresses of society. Frustrated with life on the streets in Parkersburg, West Virginia, the Kirbys found an unoccupied plot of land that is owned by the city. Tucked away between a bustling highway and quiet creek that branches off of the Ohio River, Sobriety Point’s juxtaposition provided a sense of privacy and comfort that most homeless don't get to experience on the streets in larger cities.

T

his is the house that Calvin occupied in 2015 while he made meth and led to his last drug related arrest. The Parkersburg News Sentinel reported on the drug bust, stating "Kirby was indicted on charges of conspiracy to operate or attempting to operate a clandestine drug laboratory and possession of a precursor to manufacture methamphetamine." That arrest was a catalyst for the creation of Sobriety Point.

CHAPTER TWO

SOBRIETY POINT

Listen to Calvin's thoughts about Sobriety Point.

D

riving westbound on Route 50, smoke arises from unseen chimneys. Wood-burning stoves fleetingly peak over the metal railing and are often missed by the naked eye.

Sobriety Point is a sober homeless camp where residents live a sober life. Removed from the homeless camps that don’t often encourage sober living environments, creators Calvin and Angel Kirby built Sobriety Point for themselves to live. They now live in a home and have full-time jobs; yet, the camp still stands and is open to residents willing to be sober.
