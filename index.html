SOUL OF ATHENS

RENEWAL

Sobriety Point, an encampment on the Ohio River

CHAPTER ONE

CALVIN'S JOURNEY

What led us to being homeless at first? Drug activity and drug addiction.”
-Calvin Kirby

Calvin Kirby, a 33 year old man from Huntington, West Virginia was raised in an abusive household. To tell the story of Sobriety Point, one must understand why its creator, Calvin, needed it in the first place.

Growing up, Calvin experienced a series of traumas that he says contributed to his drug addiction. His mother, who was separated from his father, was addicted to drugs and moved around frequently. Along with living in an unstable household, Calvin became the victim of rape at a young age. This left him feeling isolated and in need of help. The only way he knew how to relieve his pain was from using.

Calvin and Angel Kirby married June 20, 2011. Calvin was using when they first met and Angel was in recovery, but soon after she relapsed. Calvin says he had a temper that made him unbearable for others to be around him, which ultimately led him and Angel to homelessness. Calvin needed to be in nature, away from the stresses of society. Frustrated with life on the streets in Parkersburg, West Virginia, the Kirbys found an unoccupied plot of land that is owned by the city. Tucked away between a bustling highway and quiet creek that branches off of the Ohio River, Sobriety Point’s juxtaposition provided a sense of privacy and comfort that most homeless don't get to experience on the streets in larger cities.
