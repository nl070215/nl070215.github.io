CHAPTER ONE

CALVIN'S JOURNEY

Listen to how Calvin and Angel met.

To tell the story of Sobriety Point, one must understand why its creator, Calvin Kirby, needed it in the first place. Calvin Kirby, a 34-year-old man from Gallipolis, Ohio, had a long history of run-ins with the law and a cycle of homelessness all stemming from his drug addictions. The creation of Sobriety Point was a healing and empowering change for him and others.

Early in life, Calvin experienced instability and a series of traumas that later contributed to his drug addictions. His parents had separated early in his life and he lived with his mother who was was also an addict . The family moved often, leaving him feeling isolated and lost. He began using drugs in order to o deal with the pain of that isolation. After graduating from high school, Calvin moved to Huntington, West Virginia. Between jobs, he began selling drugs in order to survive.

What led us to being homeless at first? Drug activity and drug addiction.”


-Calvin Kirby

Calvin speaks about bringing Angel to his level through using drugs together.

(00:29)

Calvin when high once received a tattoo with his last name spelled incorrectly across his back. He plans to never cover it up because it serves as a reminder of what drugs can do to him.

On January 15, 2012, Calvin met his future wife, Angel, in Point Pleasant, West Virginia. At the time Angel was in recovery. Shortly after, however, she relapsed. In 2014, they relocated to Parkersburg, West Virginia where they moved around from friend to friend, sleeping on couches. Calvin acknowledges he had a temper that made him unbearable for others to be around, and ultimately led him and Angel to homelessness. Following two drug-related arrests, Calvin was offered the option to enter a West Virginia drug court rather than spend time in jail. The need for a stable and sober living situation became apparent. His journey towards sobriety began on November 3, 2015.

The Kirbys knew living on the street in Parkersburg would not help Calvin or Angel stay sober, they found an unoccupied plot of land owned by the city of Parkersburg. The location, tucked away between a bustling highway and quiet tributary of the Ohio River, offered Calvin and Angel refuge.

C

alvin was a part of a methamphetamine production lab that eventually led to his arrest in 2015. The Parkersburg News Sentinel reported on the police operation, stating that, "Kirby was indicted on charges of conspiracy to operate or attempting to operate a clandestine drug laboratory and possession of a precursor to manufacture methamphetamine." The arrest was a catalyst in the eventual creation of their sober encampment.

CHAPTER TWO

SOBRIETY POINT

Calvin's thoughts about Sobriety Point.

D

riving westbound on U.S. Route 50 into Parkersburg, the encampment is blink-and-you’ll miss it, the only indication something is there are the wood-burning stove chimneys that momentarily peak over the roadside railing.

Sobriety Point is the land Calvin and Angel found together on which they created a quiet sanctuary. Removed from other homeless camps that donʼt often encourage a sober living environment, they began a place where they and other residents can focus on staying clean. Calvin and Angel now live in a home and have full-time jobs, and the camp remains a place for those willing to live a sober life.

CHAPTER THREE

THE FLOOD

“It's like watching your childhood home get destroyed. It meant everything to you. Trust me, I have scars on my hands and my body from my place. I've never in my life ever forgot that place. I want to build it back.”

-Calvin Kirby

T

he river in Parkersburg, WV crested at 39.71 feet, leaving Sobriety Point completely under water on February 18, 2018. Local church groups later arrived to help tear down the old structures and prepare for a new beginning.

5 Highest Historic Crests of the Ohio River at Parkersburg, West Virginia

Those living on the banks of the Ohio River in the Parkersburg area are not strangers to flooding and the damages caused by it. On February 18, 2018, Sobriety Point was quickly submerged under water when the river crested at 39.71 feet, following several days of heavy rains. The National Weather Service reported the event as a historic crest for the region, joining a list of 72 others recorded since 1832.

CHAPTER FOUR

HOPE

Previous Next

In March 2018 Calvin had lost his full-time job due to an altercation with his boss. Calvin suffers from Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) related blackouts, which makes it difficult for him to maintain a demanding job. Moving forward, Calvin hopes to expand his current side business of custom built motorized bicycles so he earn income while being able to manage his PTSD. To supply the couple with a stable income Angel will continue to work at her current full-time job.

Its been up and down, actually the past two years have been the best of our marriage.”


-Angel Kirby, wife of Calvin, co-founder of Sobriety Point and former resident
